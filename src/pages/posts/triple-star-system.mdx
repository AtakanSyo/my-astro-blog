The Alpha Centauri star system is a hierarchical triple: 
two stars (Alpha Centauri A and B) form a tight binary at 
the center, orbiting their shared barycenter, while a third star (Proxima 
Centauri) moves on a vastly wider 
orbit around that inner pair. The inner stars behave almost like a single combined mass to the 
distant companion, which is why the outer orbit can remain stable over immense timescales.
