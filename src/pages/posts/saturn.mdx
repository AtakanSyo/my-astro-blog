<div class="p5-article">
  <h2>Saturn’s Rings at a Glance</h2>
  <p>
    Saturn’s main rings span roughly <em>1.2–2.3</em> planetary radii:
    the faint <strong>C ring</strong> inside, the bright <strong>B ring</strong>, the dark
    <strong>Cassini Division</strong>, and the outer <strong>A ring</strong> with a thin <strong>Encke gap</strong>.
    This sketch approximates their relative brightness and placement for a result that’s both <em>plausible</em> and <em>pretty</em>.
  </p>

  <h3>Ring Tilt</h3>
  <p>
    The rings are drawn as a thin, tilted plane. Adjust the tilt to see how the globe hides the back side and how
    the planet’s shadow falls across the rings—depth cues that make Saturn pop.
  </p>
