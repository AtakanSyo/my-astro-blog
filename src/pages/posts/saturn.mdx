<div class="sim-article">
  <h2 id="overview">What this Saturn simulation shows</h2>
  <p>
    This interactive model renders <strong>Saturn’s ring system</strong> with realistic scale and transparency,
    paired with a physically motivated atmosphere and dynamic rotation. It’s designed for students,
    educators, and astronomy enthusiasts who want an <strong>accurate yet responsive</strong> visualization.
  </p>

  <h3 id="ring-accuracy">Ring accuracy & proportions</h3>
  <p>
    The main rings (C, B, A) are sized using widely reported inner/outer edges:
    <em>inner ≈ 1.236× planetary radius</em> and <em>outer ≈ 2.326× planetary radius</em>.
    This captures the visual extent of Saturn’s rings while remaining performant for the web.
  </p>

  <h3 id="lighting">Lighting, shadows, and atmosphere</h3>
  <p>
    Lighting uses a fixed Sun direction for consistent shading. The simulation applies
    <strong>ACES tone mapping</strong> and a lightweight scattering term to enhance the limb, giving Saturn
    a soft, realistic terminator without heavy GPU cost.
  </p>

  <h2 id="controls-performance">Rotation, tilt, and performance</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Rotation:</strong> The planet and rings rotate together based on the configured day length.</li>
    <li><strong>Tilt:</strong> Saturn’s axial tilt can be set and animated slowly to demonstrate orientation changes.</li>
    <li><strong>Performance:</strong> Uses <strong>WebGL2</strong> with optimized textures, anisotropy, and careful shader usage.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2 id="tech-notes">Technical notes</h2>
  <p>
    Built with <strong>Three.js</strong> and <strong>WebGL2</strong>. The scene uses physically based rendering and ACES tone mapping.
    Ring opacity and color are tuned for clarity across devices. On supported browsers, enabling
    <strong>WebGPU</strong> can further improve shading precision and frame rate.
  </p>
