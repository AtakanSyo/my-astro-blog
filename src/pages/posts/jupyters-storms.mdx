<div class="p5-article">
  <h2>How Jupiter’s Bands and Storms Work</h2>
  <p>
    Jupiter’s atmosphere is organized into alternating <em>belts</em> (darker, descending air) and <em>zones</em> (brighter, rising air),
    driven by strong <strong>zonal jets</strong>. These flows shear cloud decks into the familiar stripes and spawn long-lived vortices.
  </p>

  <h3>The Great Red Spot</h3>
  <p>
    The Great Red Spot is an <strong>anticyclonic storm</strong> centered near ~22° south latitude. Its size and color vary over time,
    but it has persisted for centuries. In the sim, you’ll see it drift slowly in longitude and wobble with a noisy edge.
  </p>

  <h3>White Ovals and Smaller Storms</h3>
  <p>
    Smaller <em>white ovals</em> frequently appear at mid-latitudes. They can merge, dissipate, or migrate in the changing jet streams.
    The simulation sprinkles several ovals that advect at a faster rate than the Red Spot.
  </p>

  <h3>Performance & SEO</h3>
  <p>
    The sketch uses an off-screen texture and <code>WEBGL</code> sphere for smooth rendering. Scripts are loaded only after Start,
    minimizing initial JS and improving Core Web Vitals.
  </p>
