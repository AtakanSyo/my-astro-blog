<div class="p5-article">
  <h2>Galilean Moons</h2>
  <p>
    The four largest moons—<strong>Io</strong>, <strong>Europa</strong>, <strong>Ganymede</strong>, and <strong>Callisto</strong>—orbit in the same
    general plane as Jupiter’s equator. Inner moons zip around faster; outer moons drift more slowly.
    Trails in this sketch show each moon’s recent path to help your eyes follow the motion.
