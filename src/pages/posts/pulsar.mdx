<div class="p5-article">
  <h2>Under the hood</h2>
  <p>
    The WebGL2 path renders tens of thousands of particles as <em>instanced sprites</em> with additive blending and depth disabled—so no “TV static” at the rim.
    On WebGPU, the particle positions update in a <em>compute pass</em> and render in one instanced draw, keeping CPU overhead near zero.
  </p>

  <h3>Compatibility</h3>
  <p>
    Works on Chrome, Edge, Firefox (WebGL2). WebGPU is available on modern Chrome/Edge and recent Safari; otherwise the page stays on WebGL2.
    You can force WebGPU (when supported) by appending <code>?gpu=1</code> to the URL.
  </p>
</div>
