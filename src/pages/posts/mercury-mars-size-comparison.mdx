<h2>The numbers behind the scale</h2>
<p>Mercuryâ€™s diameter is approximately 4,880 km. Mars has a diameter of about 6,779 km. The ratio of their diameters is:</p>

$$
\frac{D_M}{D_{Me}} = \frac{6{,}779}{4{,}880} \approx 1.39
$$

<p>Volume scales with the cube of the diameter:</p>

$$
(1.39)^3 \approx 2.70
$$

<p>This means Mars has almost three times the volume of Mercury. Surface area, which determines how large each planet appears visually, scales with the square of the diameter:</p>

$$
(1.39)^2 \approx 1.93
$$
