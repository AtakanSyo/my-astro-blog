<h2>Safety and Supervision Tips</h2>

<p>
Never point any telescope at the Sun without a dedicated solar filter. Even a brief glance can cause permanent eye
damage. Set expectations about handling optics, store accessories in a small caddy, and let kids take the lead on
easy targets like the Moon, Jupiter, and the Orion Nebula.
</p>

<h2>Next Steps for Curious Families</h2>

<p>
Pair telescope nights with a star chart app, and add simple observing journals so kids can sketch what they see.
When the weather cooperates, invite friends for a “lunar open house” to build a sense of adventure that lasts well
beyond the first few sessions.
</p>
